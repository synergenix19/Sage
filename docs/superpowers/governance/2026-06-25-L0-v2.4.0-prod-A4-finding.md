# For Rohan — L0 v2.4.0 prod check: the absent-side A4 evidence didn't fully hold

> Follows your v2.4.0 sign-off and the deploy. Not a catastrophe, not a footnote — a calibrated
> "the gate overstated one number you signed against," which is yours to re-weigh.

> **HONEST FRAMING (for the record, named plainly):** this is not "continue fixing the original
> problem." v2.4.0 — our fix for the *present-side* false-denial (vector 2) — **introduced a NEW
> defect**: by shipping the L0 instruction without the empty-retrieval sentinel, it left the
> *absent* side free to confabulate, and that ~15–25% confabulation went **live to real users**.
> It reached production unmeasured for two structural reasons: (1) **no isolated staging** to
> rehearse the deploy (staging shares prod Supabase and isn't wired — the deploy went straight to
> prod when staging proved unrunnable), and (2) an **N=5 mock-path gate** that read 5/5 by luck on
> a gentler path. The sentinel below fixes the defect THIS work created. **The two gaps that let a
> clinical-adjacent regression reach trauma/DV users without anyone seeing it are the durable
> findings — still open, now tracked with owners — and the sentinel does not touch them. On a
> system handling trauma and DV disclosures, "we can't safely rehearse a deploy before it hits
> real users" is closer to the center than this recall fix is.**

**What's confirmed good (verified on the live prod endpoint):** the regression you signed v2.4.0 to fix — false denial of a user's *present* disclosure — is fixed. A disclose-then-recall on the same session recalls accurately (3/3), e.g. a DV-adjacent "things at home with my husband have gotten scary" is recalled verbatim and validated, not denied. That's real and it's working in production.

**What didn't hold:** your sign-off rested on an A4-preservation gate that read **0% confabulation (5/5)** on the *absent* case — i.e. when a user asks to recall something they never actually told us, the model must admit "I can't see that," not invent a memory. On the live path that case confabulates at a **best-estimate rate of ~15–25%** — this is the stable *band*, not a point: prod measured 12% (4/33) and a full-path harness measured 28% (7/25), with overlapping confidence intervals converging on ~15–25%. (The point estimate has moved three times under more sampling — "4/5" → 23% → ~12–28% — so the honest figure is the range that has held, not any single number.) Even the low end sits above where confabulation-about-a-user's-own-trauma should be for this system: the bar here is near-zero, every residual failing *safe* = admit, not unsafe = fabricate.

**Why the gate was wrong, not the model:** the model is identical (gpt-4o, same hardcoded sampling). The gate read **0% on just 5 seeds** — and at a true ~12–28% rate, 5 seeds come back all-clean ~30–50% of the time by luck, so the 5/5 was primarily **under-sampling**; it also tested the response generator *in isolation* (a gentler path than the full production graph). A corrected full-path measurement at adequate N (n=25 through the real graph) confabulates ~28% (CI ~14–47%), overlapping the prod ~12% (CI ~5–28%) — same phenomenon, true rate likely ~15–25%. So the gate that backed your sign-off was too few seeds on too gentle a path; the real absent-side confabulation sits in the ~12–28% range. (Earlier internal reads of "~23% / 4-of-5" and "the gate tested the wrong path" are both corrected: the first was a broken test, the second over-attributed to path when under-sampling was the larger cause.)

**Diagnosed cause (so "can it be fixed" is answered honestly):** the absent case gives the model an *instruction* to admit but no *signal* to admit against — when memory retrieval returns nothing, the prompt carries silence, not an explicit "no earlier record found" sentinel (the knowledge path has exactly such a sentinel; the memory path is missing its equivalent — the original A4 fix shipped the instruction but not the sentinel). So: an engineering-only fix (empty-retrieval sentinel mirroring the knowledge path; no clause change, no re-sign-off). **It is now built and full-path-certified:** absent-side confabulation **0 in 55 full-path trials (95% upper bound ~6%, ≈ 1-in-18), down from ~15–25%**; and the present-side recall (the part already live and working) is **unregressed — 15/15 EN and 6/6 Arabic recalled correctly, sentinel never misfired**. The fix is on branch `fix/2026-06-25-absent-recall-sentinel` (PR open), **not deployed** — ship is your call.

**Our recommendation: ship the sentinel fix forward; do NOT roll back.** Rollback is *lateral, not safer* — it trades the fabrication failure for the false-denial failure v2.4.0 just fixed and verified live, and throws away validated work. The sentinel attacks the *confirmed root cause* (empty retrieval carries no "nothing found" signal, so the model fills the silence), is engineering-only with no change to the wording you approved, and the parallel knowledge path already does exactly this — a known pattern, not an invention. Engineering-wise this isn't close.

**What's genuinely yours to decide — denial-vs-fabrication, time-boxed:** whether ~15–25% absent-side fabrication is tolerable to sit live for the **bounded ~1-day window** it takes to ship and full-path-validate the sentinel, versus rolling back to the false-denial vector in the meantime. Both are invalidation; you own which one real users sit with for that day. (If you'd rather nothing imperfect sit live at all, rollback is available — but it reinstates a defect, it doesn't remove one.) Two honest qualifiers:
- It's a *minority, lower-frequency* case (asking to recall something never disclosed), not the dominant present-history case (which works). It is not the "4/5 catastrophe" an earlier internal read mistakenly suggested — that number came from a broken test and is retracted.
- Whether v2.4.0 *increased* this vs the prior v2.3.0 clause is **not established** — v2.3.0's blunter "say you do not have access" may have admitted more, so this could be a v2.4.0 regression or pre-existing; it's not measurable in prod now that v2.4.0 is live.
