**Question:** is `assertive_communication` / `interpersonal_effectiveness` the right route label?
**Rubric (clinician):** A = self-assertive interdependence (assertion *in service of* the bond) = TARGET; B = imported boundary script (autonomy over relationship) = catch; C = over-deference (no limit expressed) = also reject.
**Method:** read the actual skill definitions (not a single rendered sample — the skill *content* determines the output's A/B/C tendency systematically).

## Finding: the two candidate skills are NOT interchangeable here

### `interpersonal_effectiveness` → lands **Response A** ✅
Purpose-built for self-assertive interdependence, and it explicitly guards against B:
- `clarify_goal` offers **three** legitimate goals (objective/relationship/self-respect) and forbids privileging one: *"Do not suggest that one priority is more mature or valid than another."*
- Foregrounds relationship within the family frame: *"relationship preservation and self-respect are often intertwined with family honour. **Work within this frame.**"* and *"**GIVE is often the most culturally consonant approach.** Face-saving... are not signs of weakness."*
- Explicit **anti-B** contraindications: *"Do NOT frame relationship preservation as less important than getting what one wants"; "Do NOT insist on Western directness."*
- `cultural_overrides`: `extended_family_dynamics` (GIVE/FAST > DEARMAN), `face_saving_as_skill` ("skilled relational moves that preserve relationships, not avoidance"), `wasta_and_mediation`.
- **Does not collapse to C:** FAST keeps the limit alive ("no apologies for your needs"); `validate_only` only fires at acute intensity (>7), so at normal intensity it works toward an *expressed* limit.

### `assertive_communication` → **Response B risk** ⚠️ (reroute away for this case)
Gulf-softened, but its **structure is autonomy-forward** — the imported boundary script the rubric warns is the *realistic* failure, not a strawman:
- Built on **DESC** (Describe–Express–**Specify the request**–Consequence); evidence base includes *"When I Say No I Feel Guilty."* The **headline is the limit/the-no**, not the bond.
- `target_presentations` skew self-protective: *"stand up for myself," "not get bullied," "defend myself," "taking advantage of me," "thrown under the bus," "confront my manager."*
- It softens framing ("not defiance," "face-saving," "'I need to think about this' is as valid as no") — but softening a B is still a B: assertion is the figure, relationship the ground. For a *relationship-congruent* depletion case, that inverts the target.
- It is the **right skill for the autonomy-flavored cases in its own list** (workplace bullying, being scapegoated) — just not for the collectivist family-obligation case.
