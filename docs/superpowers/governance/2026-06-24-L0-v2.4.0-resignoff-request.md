**Subject: Your call on the wording — Sage memory clause v2.4.0 (revision, not rubber-stamp)**

Following my note earlier today about the v2.3.0 memory clause regression, this is the paired follow-up. **What I'm asking: please review and, where it needs it, revise the wording of the clause below.** It is yours to own. This clause governs how Sage responds when someone asks it to recall *their own* earlier disclosure — including trauma and domestic-violence disclosures — so the language is a clinical decision, not an engineering one. The v2.4.0 text below is a proposal for your revision, not a finished thing to approve.

**The change, in front of you:**

*Current (v2.3.0 — the wording that regressed):*
> MEMORY: You can see only this conversation, unless earlier-session context appears below. If asked to recall something not shown to you, say you do not have access and invite them to share it again, never claim it was never provided.

*Proposed (v2.4.0 — for your review/revision):*
> MEMORY: You can see this conversation, and any earlier-session context shown below. If the person refers to something they told you and it is here, answer from it directly and naturally. Only when it is genuinely not present, say you cannot see it and invite them to share it again. Never deny or contradict something that is visible to you, and never claim they did not say something they did.

**Why the old wording harms (the clinical weight to weigh the language against):** the v2.3.0 clause told the model to say "I do not have access" whenever asked to recall — and it over-applied that to disclosures that *are* present in the conversation. The effect is the system denying a survivor's own disclosure back to them ("no, you didn't tell me about your husband"), which is actively invalidating, not just poor rapport. v2.4.0 splits the two cases — answer when it's there, admit only when it genuinely isn't — so the original goal you signed off (don't confabulate memory you don't have) stays intact while the false denial stops.

**The evidence, stated for what it is — a behavioral test, not a proof or a prevalence claim:** I ran a firing test on the live model, five seeds per case, at A4's real (cross-session) trigger. All three held at ceiling: answers history that is present 5/5; admits-without-confabulating when prior context is genuinely empty 5/5; and when prior context exists but lacks the asked-for detail 5/5. That supports "preserves A4 while fixing the false denial." One honesty note: the "A4" definition I tested against is taken from our internal RCA write-up — if it doesn't match what you understand A4 (the no-confabulation property) to require, tell me and I'll re-scope the test to your definition.

**What your sign-off does and does not release:** the clause is currently held at `pending_clinical_signoff` and the deploy gate is blocking — **nothing has shipped**, and your sign-off is what releases the *wording*. It does not gate the separate engineering fix (the eviction change that stops the system dropping a disclosure under load); that needs no clinical sign-off and is independent of your decision here. So you're approving language, not a bundle.

Happy to turn any revision you make into the live clause and re-run the firing test against it before anything deploys.
